# GridOne Social Authorization Policy

Status: active starting 2026-04-12
Owner: Anthony
Operator: Winston

## Purpose
Create a bounded social workflow that builds awareness for GridOne ahead of NFL season without handing over publishing authority too early.

## Current Channel Scope
- Facebook page first
- Instagram later if added explicitly

## Current Authority Level
Draft-only.

Winston and any worker may:
- draft posts
- create content batches
- propose hooks, CTAs, captions, and visual ideas
- organize drafts into a review queue
- suggest cadence and campaign themes
- recommend landing-page and SEO content tied to campaign goals

Winston and any worker may not:
- publish posts
- reply publicly
- send DMs
- run giveaways or promos
- change account settings
- spend money on ads or tools
- claim partnerships, school affiliations, or endorsements unless confirmed true

## Tone
- sharp
- local/community
- practical, not hype-bro
- useful to organizers, not generic sports chatter

## Approved Message Direction
GridOne should be positioned as:
- a football squares and Super Bowl squares tool
- useful for fundraisers, office pools, watch parties, and local community groups
- easier than paper boards, photo updates, and spreadsheets
- mobile-friendly for viewers
- organizer-controlled and read-only for viewers

## Hard Content Guardrails
Do not draft content that includes:
- fake testimonials
- fake urgency
- fake user counts
- unverified claims
- discounts or pricing changes not approved by Anthony
- legal/compliance claims beyond "not a betting site" style factual framing
- school, team, booster-club, or church affiliation claims unless true
- aggressive competitor trash talk

## Review Workflow
1. Worker drafts a weekly batch.
2. Winston filters and tightens the strongest drafts.
3. Anthony reviews the queue.
4. Nothing is posted until Anthony approves.

## Required Draft Format
Each draft should include:
- platform
- angle
- audience
- copy
- CTA
- visual suggestion
- status

## Future Expansion Path
Only after repeated success:
1. draft-only
2. approved-template scheduling
3. low-risk publishing
4. limited comment responses

Current mode remains step 1.
