# GridOne Launch Social Pack

Status: draft-only, ready for Anthony review
Primary channel: Facebook first
Secondary use: website/blog snippets, local group posting, simple image/carousel briefs

## Recommendation
Start with fundraiser-first positioning, not generic football-fan positioning.

Why:
- it gives GridOne a clearer painkiller story
- it fits the product better than broad entertainment language
- it creates a more credible local/community angle for Facebook

## Core content pillars
1. Fundraiser pain removal
2. Organizer simplicity and trust
3. Game-day energy and live viewer experience

## Launch-week cadence
- Monday: fundraiser pain-point post
- Wednesday: how-it-works post or carousel
- Friday: office/community use-case post
- Saturday or Sunday: game-day energy post or poll

## 5 engagement prompts
- Do you usually run football squares on paper or digitally?
- What is the most annoying part of organizing a squares pool?
- Booster clubs: would one live board link help your fundraiser?
- Office pools: what usually causes the most confusion on game day?
- What game-day update do people ask for most: winners, payouts, or next-score scenarios?

## Comment-response playbook
- Keep replies short and human
- Answer questions directly
- Do not drop links unless the context clearly supports it
- Avoid argument threads
- If someone asks pricing: "This season, $14.99 unlocks at least 20 boards once you're ready to share."
- If someone asks what it is: "GridOne is a football squares app that gives organizers one clean live board link for fundraisers, office pools, and watch parties."
- If someone asks about launch: "We’re getting the site and content dialed in now. Happy to keep you posted."

## Creative briefs
- Post 1 image: paper-board chaos vs clean phone mockup
- Post 2 image: mobile board screenshot in office setting
- Post 3 image: collage of booster club, watch party, and community fundraiser scenes
- Post 4 carousel: build, clean up, share, follow live
- Post 5 image: organizer dashboard or live winner scenario view

## Group-posting guardrails
- no bulk posting
- no copy-paste spam across groups
- prioritize relevant local/community groups
- lead with usefulness, not a hard sell
- if a group forbids promo, do not force it
